Top 5 Taylor Swift Eras

Taylor Swift's Eras Tour has proven to be a great sucess, both for her fans and general audiences. In ten sections, Taylor takes her audience through over a decade of music. But some, like myself, have their favorite eras. Below, I will be showing my own ranking.

1, 2, 3, Let's Go, Bitch!

Number 5: Midnights

In 5th place is Midnights, which at the time of writing is the most recent non-rerecording from Taylor Swift. (In less than a month, The Tortured Poets Department will be released, less than two years after Midnights.) The songs in this section of the show are:

  • Anti‚ÄêHero
  • Midnight Rain
  • Vigilante Shit
  • Bejeweled
  • Mastermind
  • Karma

When I went to see The Eras Tour, Ice Spice appeared during the final number. While I like all these songs, making this set the last of the night was just a bit too overwhelming for me.


Midnights, which closes out the concert, has since won Album of the Year, Swift's fourth.


The song Nothing New features Phoebe Bridgers, who opened for Taylor Swift at select locations.

Number 4: Red

Next up is Red, first released in 2012, and re-released in 2022. The songs in this set include:

  • 22
  • We are Never Ever Getting Back Together
  • I Knew You Were Trouble
  • Nothing New
  • All Too Well (10 Minute Version)

These songs are some of the most nostalgic for me in the Eras Tour, since I was pretty young when the original album came out and it has been a mainstay in my life. While not part of this set, one of the suprise songs when I saw the show was from Red, and that song (Holy Ground) is one of my favorites from the album. While All Too Well drags on sometimes, it is a magical thing to experience with others.

Number 3: Folklore

In third place is Folklore, the first of two pandemic-era albums. Folklore is defined by woodsy visuals and its fictional characters, a first for Taylor Swift's albums. The songs in this setlist are:

  • The 1
  • Betty
  • The Last Great American Dynasty
  • August
  • Illicit Affairs
  • My Tears Ricochet
  • Cardigan

These songs were great to hear live, since they had never been performed live before the Eras Tour. I especially loved August, and it was really amazing to see it live.


The Folklore set had some changes, including switching out "Invisible String" for "The 1."

Number 2: Fearless

This album is my favorite out of all 11, which honestly is the reason this set is so high up. You Belong with Me was such a formative song in my childhood, and I am a bit sad that the original album is no longer in vogue. The songs on this portion of the setlist are:

  • Fearless
  • You Belong with Me
  • Love Story

I am more than a little disappointed that this set is so short, though it is not the shortest set of the night. Speak Now only has one song, which is one of many reasons that era did not make my top five. The three songs that Taylor sings for the Fearless set are some of her most sing-able, which makes this early set a really fun time.


The hand heart is an iconic symbol of the Fearless era.


Taylor Swift's Lover album opens the concert and was the first under her new record label.

Number 1: Lover

She's Miss Americana, after all. Lover, the first of Taylor's albums with Republic Records, was also the first of her albums not to get a tour since the initial debut album (also called Taylor Swift!) Thus, she knew she had to make a splash with this album's setlist. And a splash she did make! The Lover setlist includes:

  • Miss Americana & the Heartbreak Prince
  • Cruel Summer
  • The Man
  • You Need to Calm Down
  • Lover
  • The Archer

This era opens the concert, which was a surprise when I first read about the setlist. However, I think Miss Americana & the Heartbreak Prince is a great opener. Cruel Summer is a fan favorite, for good reason. I only wish that my favorite song from the album, Paper Rings, made the setlist.
